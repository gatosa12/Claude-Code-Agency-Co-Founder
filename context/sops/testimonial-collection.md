# Testimonial Collection SOP

How to get testimonials from your voice AI clients. Testimonials are your most powerful sales tool -- a 2-minute video from a dental office owner saying "the AI agent booked 30 appointments in the first month" is worth more than any pitch deck.

## 1. When to Ask

Timing is everything. Ask too early and you get a vague "it's been fine." Ask at the right moment and you get a compelling story with real numbers.

### Good Times to Ask

- **After a measurable win.** The client says "conversion rate doubled" or "we haven't missed a call in 3 weeks." Strike while the iron is hot.
- **After spontaneous praise.** They say "this is working great" or "my staff loves this." Follow up immediately: "Would you be open to sharing that feedback as a quick testimonial?"
- **At the 30-day mark.** If results are positive and the client is happy, the one-month milestone is a natural time to ask.
- **At the 60-day mark.** If 30 days felt too early, 60 days gives you stronger data and a more established relationship.
- **After a client referral.** If they refer someone to you unprompted, they are clearly satisfied. Ask for a formal testimonial while they are in referral mode.

### Bad Times to Ask

- **During onboarding.** They have not seen results yet. Wait.
- **After a bug or outage.** Even if it is resolved, the timing feels tone-deaf. Wait at least 2 weeks.
- **During a scope dispute.** If you are navigating a scope creep conversation, asking for a testimonial will feel manipulative.
- **Before results are in.** Never ask "how do you like it so far?" in week one. You need real data to back up any testimonial.

## 2. How to Ask

Keep it casual. Do not make it feel like a corporate request.

### In a Meeting (Best Approach)

At the end of a check-in where the client has shared positive results, say:

> "That's great to hear. Would you be open to sharing a quick testimonial about your experience? It really helps other [dental offices / roofing companies / med spas] understand what's possible with voice AI. I can make it super easy -- just a couple of minutes."

### Over Email (If You Missed the Window in Person)

Subject: Quick favor

> "Hi [name], I loved hearing about the [specific result] during our call. Would you be open to sharing a quick testimonial about your experience? I can send you 3 questions to answer, or if you'd prefer, I can draft something based on our conversations and you just approve it. Either way, it would take less than 5 minutes. Let me know what works best for you."

### Key Principles

- **Make it easy.** The lower the effort, the higher the chance they say yes.
- **Be specific about what you need.** "A quick testimonial" is better than "some feedback." Give them a format.
- **Give them an out.** "Only if you're comfortable" removes pressure and paradoxically increases acceptance.
- **Offer to do the work.** "I can draft it and you just approve" removes their biggest objection (time).

## 3. Formats

Listed from lowest effort to highest. Start with whatever the client is most comfortable with.

### Written Quote (Lowest Effort)

- **How:** You draft a 2-3 sentence quote based on their actual words from meetings. Send it to them for approval.
- **Example:** "Since [agency name] set up our voice AI agent, we've booked 30 more appointments per month without adding front desk staff. The agent handles after-hours calls that we used to lose completely. It paid for itself in the first week." -- Dr. Sarah Chen, Sunrise Dental
- **Best for:** Clients who are busy, camera-shy, or private. Use in proposals, outreach emails, and your website.
- **Tip:** Pull exact phrases from your meeting notes. If they said "it paid for itself in the first week," use those words.

### Video Call Recording (Medium Effort)

- **How:** Record a 2-3 minute segment during a Zoom call. Ask the 3 questions from Section 5. The informal setting makes it feel natural, not scripted.
- **Best for:** Social media posts, website hero section, outreach DMs. Video testimonials convert 2-3x better than written ones.
- **Tip:** Tell them "this will take literally 2 minutes, I'll ask 3 quick questions." Then start recording before they overthink it.

### Case Study (Highest Effort -- You Do the Work)

- **How:** You write a 1-page case study based on the client's data and your meeting notes. Include: the problem, the solution, the results (with numbers), and a client quote. Send it to the client for review and approval.
- **Best for:** Proposals to similar businesses, website portfolio, Skool community posts.
- **Tip:** Case studies are the most valuable format but require the most data. Wait until you have at least 30 days of production metrics.

## 4. The Free Pilot Exchange

This strategy is specifically for agencies with 0-3 clients who need their first testimonials to build credibility.

### The Offer

"I'll set up the voice AI system for free for 2 weeks. If it meets the agreed performance metrics, all I ask in return is a 2-minute video testimonial about your experience."

### How to Structure It

1. **Find the right candidate.** Look for a business that:
   - Has a clear pain point (missed calls, slow lead response).
   - Is in your target niche (so the testimonial is relevant to future prospects).
   - Has a decision maker who is open to video.
   - Has enough call volume to see results in 2 weeks.

2. **Set clear metrics upfront.** Define what success looks like before you start:
   - "If the agent books at least 10 appointments in 2 weeks..."
   - "If the agent answers 95% of after-hours calls..."
   - "If the lead response time drops below 60 seconds..."

3. **Put it in writing.** Even for a free pilot, send a brief agreement:
   - What you will deliver (specific use case, configured and tested).
   - What metrics define success.
   - What they agree to provide if metrics are met (video testimonial, 2-3 minutes, answering 3 specific questions).
   - Timeline (2 weeks from go-live).

4. **Deliver like a paying client.** Do not cut corners because it is free. This is your showcase project.

5. **At the 2-week mark:** Present the results. If metrics were met, record the testimonial right then on the same call. Do not schedule it for later -- momentum matters.

6. **Convert to paying client.** After the testimonial is recorded, present your pricing: "Now that you've seen the results, here's what it looks like to continue. I recommend the [tier] plan based on your volume."

### Why This Works

- You get a real testimonial with real data, not a fake review.
- The client gets to try before they buy, removing risk.
- If it works, they almost always convert to a paying client.
- Even if they do not convert, you have a testimonial and a case study.

## 5. Testimonial Questions

When recording a video or gathering a written quote, ask these three questions in this order:

1. **"What was your biggest challenge with [missed calls / lead response / after-hours coverage] before we set up the voice agent?"**
   - This establishes the "before" state. You want them to describe the pain in their own words.
   - Follow up if needed: "How was that affecting your business?"

2. **"What results have you seen since the voice agent went live?"**
   - This is where the numbers come in. Prompt them if needed: "How many appointments has it booked? What's the call volume like?"
   - Let them talk. The more specific they are, the better the testimonial.

3. **"What would you tell another [dental office / roofing company / med spa] owner who's considering voice AI?"**
   - This is the money question. It turns them into an advocate speaking directly to your future prospects.
   - Often produces the best soundbites: "Stop overthinking it and just try it."

## 6. Using Testimonials

Once you have testimonials, put them to work across every sales channel.

1. **In proposals.** Add a testimonial section to every proposal. Include the most relevant testimonial for the prospect's industry. Run `/agency-ops:proposal` and include testimonial data in your input.
2. **In outreach messages.** When running `/agency-ops:outreach`, reference testimonials in cold messages: "We helped [similar business] book 30 extra appointments per month with a voice AI agent. Here's what their owner said: [quote]."
3. **On social media.** Post video testimonials on LinkedIn, Instagram, and in the Skool community. Tag the client (with permission). Short clips (30-60 seconds) from the full testimonial work best.
4. **On your website.** If you have an agency website, add a testimonials/results section.
5. **In discovery calls.** When a prospect asks "have you done this before?" you can say "yes, here's what [client name] experienced" and share the case study or video link.

### Permission Checklist

Before using any testimonial publicly:
- [ ] Client approved the exact text/video being used.
- [ ] Client approved the channels where it will be shared (proposals only, social media, website, etc.).
- [ ] If using their business name and photo/video, you have explicit written permission.
- [ ] If they requested anonymity, use "dental practice owner in [city]" instead of their name.
